The maths this runs on

  • Position sizing: units = (0.5% of balance) ÷ stop distance. The stop is 1.8×ATR(14) below entry, attached to every order at the broker. One losing trade costs ≈ 0.5% of the account, by construction.
  • Expectancy (the number that decides everything): mean profit per trade in R, where 1R = the amount risked. Walk-forward out-of-sample estimate: +0.273R over 198 trades (PF 1.56) — treated as an optimistic upper bound, since the strategy structure was shaped on the same dataset. This forward test is the honest measurement.
  • Trade management: at +1R the stop moves to break-even; no trailing stop (testing showed trails strangled the winners); everything closes by 20:45 UTC — no overnight exposure.
  • Hard limits: max 1 position; −2% day stops trading for the day; −5% week stops the week. Sizing never depends on previous losses — no martingale, no averaging down, structurally.
  • Expected drawdown (Monte Carlo, 1,000 reshuffles of the OOS trades): ~5% median, ~8% at the 95th percentile. Beyond ~9% means the model is wrong — halt and review.
